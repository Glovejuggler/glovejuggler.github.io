Point of Sales (POS) System

Overview

A web-based POS system built specifically for UPTOP Diner, designed to streamline restaurant operations from order placement to kitchen preparation. The system manages sales, tracks inventory, and updates kitchen orders in real time, ensuring smooth coordination between the dining area and the kitchen.

It’s built to handle the fast pace of restaurant service — from taking customer orders efficiently to monitoring stock usage and generating daily sales summaries.

Tech Used: Laravel, Vue.js (Composition API), Inertia.js, MySQL, Axios, Chart.js, Tailwind CSS

Key Features

  • Order Management: Handle dine-in and takeout orders with instant updates.
  • Kitchen Display Integration: Real-time synchronization between cashier and kitchen staff for faster service.
  • Cost & Ingredient Tracking: Calculates ingredient usage and meal costing based on sales, without managing full stock levels.
  • Product & Menu Management: Add and update menu items with pricing, categories, and availability status.
  • Reports & Analytics: Generate sales summaries, top-selling items, and daily revenue insights.
  • Dashboard: Visual analytics for weekly sales and number of orders.

The Story Behind It

This project was developed for UPTOP Diner, a local restaurant that needed a POS solution tailored to their workflow. The goal was to replace manual order slips and delays in the kitchen with a digital, synchronized system. By creating a real-time connection between front-of-house and kitchen operations, the system reduced errors, improved speed, and helped the team deliver a smoother dining experience.

What I'm Proud Of

Building this POS System taught me how to design a real-time, event-driven workflow — where every click at the counter instantly reflects on another screen. It deepened my understanding of data synchronization, efficient frontend communication, and how technology can make everyday service faster and more reliable.
